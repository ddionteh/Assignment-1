Philosopher of the Day

Aristotle

Birth: 384 BCE
Death: 322 BCE

Famous Quote: "No great mind has ever existed without a touch of madness."

Description:Aristotle, born in 384 BCE, was a Greek philosopher and polymath who significantly influenced Western thought. A student of Plato, he later tutored Alexander the Great. Aristotle's works encompass diverse subjects, including ethics, metaphysics, politics, and natural sciences. He emphasized empirical observation and logical reasoning, pioneering systematic approaches to knowledge. In ethics, Aristotle proposed virtue ethics, focusing on character development and moral virtues. His metaphysics explored the nature of reality, formulating concepts like substance and potentiality. In politics, he wrote "Politics," advocating for a balanced, virtuous government. Aristotle's contributions laid foundations for various disciplines, making him a key figure in the history of philosophy and science. He passed away in 322 BCE.
